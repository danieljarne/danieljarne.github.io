Daniel Jarne Ornia
I am a researcher in AI for control and multi-agent systems. I obtained a PhD at Delft University of Technology (Delft Center for Systems and Control) working on swarm robotics, multi-agent systems and formal methods for learning-based control. I am currently a postdoc researcher at the Computer Science department at the University of Oxford, working on prediction, learning and control for large agent based models (ABMs).

Personally, I spent most of my free time surfing, snowboarding or climbing, I enjoy playing music and I speak a bunch of languages1.

Research

I completed my PhD in Delft under the supervision of Dr Manuel Mazo in 2023. During these years I spent the summer of 2022 at the University of Oxford, working with Prof. Alessandro Abate on robust reinforcement learning problems. My research has ranged from mean field approximations for stochastic systems (paper) to communication-induced uncertainty in multi-agent RL (paper) and verifiable robustness in RL (paper).
Nowadays I am interested in problems related to decision making under uncertainty, information theoretic control and learning for large ABMs.
